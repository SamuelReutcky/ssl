About SSL

What is SSL?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a technology that keeps your internet connection secure. It makes sure that any data sent between your computer and a website is private and protected. When you see a padlock icon in the address bar of your browser, it means SSL is being used to keep your information safe.

What is TLS?

TLS (Transport Layer Security) is a technology that keeps your data safe when you use the internet. It works by encrypting the information, making it hard for anyone else to see or change it. You often use TLS when browsing websites, sending emails, and other online activities.

What does it mean to be secure?

To stay secure online, use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA). Look for HTTPS and the padlock icon before entering sensitive info on websites. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks, or use a VPN. Be cautious of phishing scams and keep your software updated. These steps help keep your personal information safe.

How can I tell that my webpage is secure?

To check if a webpage is secure, look for these signs:

  1. HTTPS: The web address should start with “https://” instead of “http://”. The “s” stands for secure.
  2. Padlock Icon: There should be a padlock icon in the address bar. Clicking on it will give you more details about the site’s security.
  3. Security Certificate: Click on the padlock icon to view the SSL certificate details. This shows that the site is verified and secure

What is encryption and why do I care?

Encryption turns your data into a secret code to keep it safe from hackers and unauthorized access. It ensures that only you and the intended recipient can read the information. This is important because it protects your personal details, like passwords and credit card numbers, making your online activities more secure and private. By using encryption, you can have peace of mind knowing your sensitive information is protected.

Do the prefixes HTTP or HTTPS matter when we're talking about secure websites?

Yes, the prefixes HTTP and HTTPS are important for secure websites. HTTP sends data as plain text, which isn’t secure. HTTPS adds encryption, making the data private and secure. So, when you see HTTPS in the web address, it means the website is protecting your information, making it safer to use.

What is an SSL certificate authority?

An SSL certificate authority is a trusted organization that gives out digital certificates to websites. These certificates prove that the website is secure and that its identity has been verified.
